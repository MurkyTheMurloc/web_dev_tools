# Taste Review

Use this checklist when a UI is technically fine but still feels generic, cheap,
overstimulating, or emotionally flat.

## Quick Questions

- What is the first thing the eye notices?
- Is that the right thing?
- Does the screen feel like a product or a component demo?
- Does the visual language match the job of the screen?
- If all branding disappeared, would the UI still feel chosen?

## Check Typography

- Are there clear text tiers with distinct jobs?
- Is the font choice helping the mood, or just filling space?
- Are headings and labels emotionally aligned with the product?
- Is too much hierarchy being expressed through color instead of type and space?

## Check Spacing And Rhythm

- Do sections breathe?
- Are gaps intentional or just inherited from random utility values?
- Is there a repeatable spacing rhythm?
- Does mobile still feel composed, or only compressed?

## Check Surfaces

- Are cards, borders, shadows, and backgrounds all competing?
- Does every container need to exist?
- Is the surface system coherent?
- Would removing one layer make the design stronger?

## Check Color

- Is there one primary accent role?
- Are neutrals carrying enough of the interface?
- Is any bright color doing real communication work?
- Would slightly quieter tones feel more expensive and focused?

## Check Distinctiveness

- What is the one memorable move on the page?
- Is it strong enough to notice?
- Are there too many competing “special” moments?
- Does any section still feel like starter-template UI?

## Check Restraint

- Is motion reinforcing hierarchy or just showing off?
- Are blur, glow, gradients, and shadows stacked together?
- Are rounded corners used consistently or indiscriminately?
- Is the design trying too hard to feel premium?

## Preferred Fix Order

1. fix hierarchy
2. fix spacing rhythm
3. simplify surfaces
4. rebalance color
5. refine typography
6. add or sharpen one distinctive move

Do not start with decorative effects.
